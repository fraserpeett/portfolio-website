What I'm about

In 2016, I had a bit of a career time-out forced on me by mother nature. It gave me the time I needed to reflect, reset and plan for the future (once we’d established that I had one).

One of the key reflections was that somewhere along the way, I’d lost my sense of curiosity. The world was still a wonderful place, but I was in a bit of a ‘seen-that-done-that’ mindset. Suddenly, I could feel time slipping through my fingers like fine sand and I was just watching it go. It was an awakening.

Having briefly dabbled with religion (any) with the hope of breaking down my logical approach to life, I decided to get my learning head on and get back to school. I studied Psychology so long ago that Freud could have been my tutor, but the building blocks were still there and my re-found curiosity took me on a long and winding road to UX. Oh … my … word, what kind of amazing world is this that’s been hidden from me for so long?
